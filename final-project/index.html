Current Writing Advances

Before Common Era: (BCE)

Tokens were used as the initial writing device, where they commonly took the shapes of circles, or cones. They were used to keep track of various stocked goods, as well as trades amongst other people. They commonly used symbols to communicate however, instead of the modern day method of words.

Logograms were used as a means of communication around 3000 BC. They took similar ideas of the writing token, where they’d use symbols to communicate certain topics. Think Egyptian Hieroglyphics, and the modern day Dollar Sign as examples of Logograms.

The Phoenician Abjad would be created by this time, and is credited as the inspiration for future alphabets. It had about 21 characters, with each of them alone being used to represent a word. For example, our modern day 'N' was called Nun, and meant fish.

Common Era: (CE)

Graphite Pencils, one of the first of its kind, was originally named Black Lead Pencils. They were originally used by artists to create quick sketches, before moving to the Modern Day usage we currently see and use them for.

Approximately in 1867, the first edition of the typewriter was created, which was a machine dedicated to typing long sequences of sentences within moments. They required patience and delicacy, as one could easily mess up an entire paper by accident with one mis-click.

By 1884, Fountain Pens would also start to appear. They are special pens with metal nibs at the tip, and use water based inks.Those with these kinds of pens were credited with excellent penmanship, and they are commonly used in arts such as calligraphy.

By now, the Xerographic Copier was created. It’s a system that took graphic matter, and copied it on a photoconductive insulating surface. The paper it used to do this was smoother, and perfect to hold the necessary inks of any typed documents.

Google Docs, a digital source of writing documents, was originally called Writely in 2005, is still widely used today by businesses and scholars, after its immense amount of updates, including allowing other scholars/business associates to edit and watch over a document.

The Kobo eReader and Kobowriter are devices that were created with the help of Github, a website dedicated to working with businesses and classes in the creation of various softwares. These products would skillfully replicate the fonts found on paper documents, and the typical feel of simply writing a book.

What is T.I.P.S?

T.I.P.S, which stands for The Imaginative Pen for Scholars, is a writing pencil, but with a twist; Not only does it have a wide variety of features, but it also pays you to write using it! I think this unique spin on the Writing Pen can inspire teenagers to pick up a pencil and write their hearts out! Below this mini paragraph are 3 different cards. They each talk about different parts of T.I.P.S:

T.I.P.S' Key Features:

  • T.I.P.S can be used on any surface, including more soft surfaces!
  • T.I.P.S pays the user 20 Cents for every word they write. The payment could come in the mail with a weekly check, or can be sent directly into a bank account if the user has a credit/debit card.
  • T.I.P.S will automatically disable itself upon detecting that it's being illegaly used on private property.

T.I.P.S' Power System:

  • T.I.P.S relies on 2 Big Power Sources: Batteries, and Friction.
  • When Relying on Battery, be Mindful that T.I.P.S takes 2 Triple A Batteries. You can insert them by unscrewing the 'eraser' on the pen.
  • When Relying on Friction, place your hands on the built in grips, and gently start to rub the Pen. It'd take about a Minute and 50 Seconds for the Pen to be charged up halfway, and 3 Minutes for the Pen to be fully charged.

T.I.P.S' Accessibility Systems:

  • T.I.P.S can easily be customized to display written characters in any written language.
  • T.I.P.S comes with a built in speaker, allowing writers to listen to what they wrote. This voice can also be customized in order to account for noise sensitivity.
  • T.I.P.S has customizable ink colours, and each ink colour is environmental friendly, with it coming from natural sources such as bugs, plants and the soil.
